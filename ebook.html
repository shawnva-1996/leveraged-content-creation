Chapter 1: What is a Prompt?

(And Why It's More Than Just a Question)

Many people approach AI like a simple search engine, typing in basic questions and getting generic answers. This is a fundamental misunderstanding. A prompt is not just a question; it's a command, a set of instructions, and a creative brief all rolled into one.

Deconstructing the myth: It's not magic, it's a conversation. Think of interacting with an AI as briefing a highly intelligent but very literal-minded intern. If you give vague instructions, you'll get a vague result. But if you provide clear, detailed instructions, you'll get precisely what you asked for. The magic isn't in the AI; it's in the quality of your prompt.

Garbage In, Garbage Out. This is the golden rule of prompt engineering. The AI doesn't "know" what you want unless you tell it. The time you invest in crafting a well-structured prompt will save you hours of editing and frustration later. A great prompt is the difference between a useless paragraph and a perfect piece of marketing copy.

The Goal: Turning the AI into your expert intern who works 24/7. When you master prompting, you are no longer just a user; you are a manager. You are directing an infinitely knowledgeable and tireless resource to work for you, creating content, drafting emails, and building business strategies while you focus on the big picture.

Chapter 2: The 5 Core Components of a Perfect Prompt

To get consistently great results, a powerful prompt should contain five key elements. We use the acronym PTCFT to remember them.

P for Persona

Always start by telling the AI who it should be. Giving it a role focuses its knowledge and style. Instead of a generic response, you get an expert's perspective.
Example: "Act as a world-class Singaporean food blogger..."

T for Task

Be explicit and direct about what you want the AI to do. Use action verbs. The clearer the task, the better the output.
Example: "...write three engaging hooks for a TikTok video about a new hawker stall."

C for Context

This is where you provide the crucial details. What is the product? Who is the audience? What is the unique selling proposition? The more relevant context you provide, the more tailored the response will be.
Example: "The stall sells modern laksa with a secret, home-made rempah. The target audience is young foodies aged 20-35."

F for Format

Tell the AI exactly how you want the output structured. Do you want a list? A table? A paragraph? A script? Don't leave it to chance.
Example: "Format the output as a numbered list."

T for Tone

Define the personality of the writing. This ensures the content connects with your intended audience.
Example: "The tone should be witty, informal, and exciting."

Chapter 3: 5 'Copy-Paste' Prompts for a Viral Video Script

Here are five powerful prompt templates based on the PTCFT framework. Use them as a starting point to generate your first video script.

1. The "Problem/Agitate/Solve" Hook Prompt:
Act as a scriptwriter for a viral TikTok video. Your task is to write a 3-part video hook using the Problem-Agitate-Solve formula. The context is [Your Product/Service] for [Your Audience]. The tone should be dramatic and relatable.

2. The "Storytelling" Prompt:
Act as a master storyteller. Your task is to write a short, compelling narrative script for a 30-second video. The story should be about a character who struggled with [The Problem] and overcame it using [Your Solution]. The tone should be inspirational.

3. The "Top 3 Mistakes" Prompt:
Act as an expert in [Your Niche]. Your task is to create a script for a video titled "Top 3 Mistakes People Make When [Doing Something Related to Your Niche]". For each mistake, briefly explain why it's a problem. The tone should be authoritative and helpful.

4. The "Myth vs. Fact" Prompt:
Act as a myth-buster. Your task is to write a video script that debunks a common myth about [Your Topic]. Start with the myth, then dramatically reveal the fact. The tone should be surprising and informative.

5. The "Call to Action" Prompt:
Act as a persuasive marketing expert. Your task is to write a clear and compelling call to action for the end of a video. The context is that the viewer has just learned about [Your Topic]. The call to action is to [Your Desired Action]. The tone should be urgent but friendly.

Chapter 4: The "Chain of Thought" Technique

Sometimes, for more complex tasks, you need the AI to "think" before it writes. The "Chain of Thought" technique is a simple but incredibly powerful way to improve the quality and logic of your outputs.

Instead of asking for the final output directly, you first ask the AI to break down its process.

How to do it: Simply add the phrase "First, think step-by-step about how you will accomplish this task. Then, write the final output." to your prompt.

This forces the AI to outline its logic, identify key points, and structure its response more coherently before generating the final text. It’s a simple trick that dramatically reduces errors and produces more nuanced, well-reasoned content.
